**Claim:** The Claimant is a public limited company operating approximately 180 retail stores in the United Kingdom and an online sales channel.

### Over-Extrapolation Risks

### Recommended Action

Retain only if this is intended as uncontroversial background, but obtain and cite direct corporate or business records proving plc status, UK store count, and online channel. Without that, the allegation should be treated as weakly supported rather than robustly proved.

## C006: Unsupported (8/100)

**Claim:** It was an express term of the MSA and the SOW that the go-live date for the Platform was fixed at 1 October 2024.

### Supporting Evidence

- `E003` (0.25): The MSA refers to the target go-live date stated in the SOW, confirming the date was part of the contractual framework, but not as a fixed date.

### Challenging Evidence
- `E004` (0.99): Clause 2.1 of the SOW expressly says the go-live date of 1 October 2024 'is a target date' and the implementation plan is a planning estimate subject to change control. That directly contradicts any pleading that it was fixed.
- `E003` (0.99): Clause 3.1 of the MSA states the Supplier shall use reasonable endeavours to achieve the target go-live date in the SOW and that the timetable is a planning estimate adjustable through change control. This is inconsistent with a fixed express term.
- `E007` (0.96): Change Order No. 3 expressly revises the go-live date from 1 October 2024 to 18 November 2024 for the Platform as a whole, reinforcing that the date was not fixed and was capable of contractual variation.
- `E009` (0.82): The October 2024 email thread discusses proceeding to go-live on 18 November 2024 and even a possible deferral to January 2025, consistent with a movable target date rather than a fixed contractual deadline.

### Missing Evidence
- Any contractual wording expressly stating that 1 October 2024 was a fixed or guaranteed go-live date rather than a target date.
- Any pleaded or documentary basis showing that 'fixed' is being used in some special sense despite the express wording 'target date'.
- Any later signed variation reverting the go-live date back to a fixed 1 October 2024 obligation.

### Over-Extrapolation Risks
- Conflating the presence of a date in the timetable with an express fixed contractual obligation.
- Ignoring the express contractual language in both MSA and SOW that the timetable was a planning estimate and the date a target.
- Overlooking that the change-control documents and later correspondence show the date was in fact revised, which undermines any assertion of fixity.

### Legal Explanation

On the provided evidence, this claim is not robust. Under English contractual interpretation, the court would start with the actual words used in the MSA and SOW. E003 mirrors that by requiring only reasonable endeavours to achieve the target go-live date and acknowledging timetable adjustment through signed change control. E007 then shows that the date was in fact revised to 18 November 2024 by signed Change Order, which is wholly inconsistent with a fixed express term at 1 October 2024. The claim therefore materially overstates what the contracts say. At most, the evidence supports a pleaded case that 1 October 2024 was the original target go-live date in the contractual documents, not that it was fixed.

### Recommended Action

Do not plead this as a fixed express term. Amend the allegation to say that the original SOW identified 1 October 2024 as the target go-live date, subject to change control, and that it was later revised by Change Order No. 3 to 18 November 2024.
